## Why Marketers Are Even Having This Conversation

The question of advertising inside AI assistants has moved from theoretical to operational — not because the channel is ready, but because the user attention is already there.

ChatGPT handles well over 100 million queries daily. When a platform reaches that kind of daily active usage, monetisation becomes structurally inevitable. The only real question is timing and format.

Marketers who are paying attention now are not doing so because they believe the channel is mature. They are doing so because channels that catch teams off-guard tend to be expensive to enter late. The TikTok lesson is still fresh for most growth departments.

## What the Early Signals Suggest

The signals coming from the industry are directional, not definitive. Credible market chatter suggests some brands are in early conversations about what commerce integrations and experimental ad formats inside conversational AI environments might look like.

Some programmatic players appear to be exploring how existing infrastructure could connect to AI assistant inventory. A small number of brands in high-consideration verticals — home, lifestyle, weddings — are reportedly running early tests to understand the format before it becomes standardised.

None of this is a launched, scaled product. What it is, is a formative period. The brands that ran early TikTok campaigns in 2019 when inventory was cheap and the format was undefined came out of that period with a knowledge advantage. The same dynamic applies here.

## What Is Not Confirmed

This section matters more than the previous one for most marketing and finance teams.

There is no verified, publicly documented programmatic ad product inside ChatGPT or any major AI assistant at scale. There are no published performance benchmarks — no CPA data, no conversion rate references, no audited ROAS figures. The brand safety controls that allow advertisers to verify where and how their brand appears within a conversation do not appear to exist at the standard performance marketing teams require.

Any internal proposal framing conversational AI as an active performance channel today is ahead of what the evidence supports. Treat current spend in this space as brand learning, not performance investment.

## Why This Matters Strategically

The strategic question is not whether to advertise inside an AI assistant today. It is whether the underlying user behavior pattern justifies serious monitoring and preparation.

The intent signal inside a conversational AI query is structurally different from anything else in the current media landscape. When someone asks an AI assistant to recommend a project management tool, a travel destination, or a home appliance, they are describing their problem in natural language at the exact moment they are trying to solve it.

That is richer than a keyword. It is richer than a social signal. It is as close to a purchasing conversation as digital advertising has ever had access to.

If that intent layer becomes commercially accessible at scale with proper measurement, the implications for performance marketing channel mix are significant.

## The Google Angle

Google leadership has signalled publicly that aggressive ad monetisation inside Gemini could undermine user trust. For a company whose revenue architecture depends almost entirely on advertising, that is not a throwaway comment.

The most useful interpretation: the technical and trust challenges of embedding advertising into conversational AI interfaces are harder than they appear. Ads inside a chat are not structurally equivalent to ads inside a search results page. The dynamic, context-dependent nature of a conversation makes targeting, safety, and attribution substantially more complex.

If the company with the most advertising infrastructure in the world is moving deliberately, that is a signal worth taking seriously. It does not mean the channel is unviable — it means the timeline is longer and the complexity is higher than optimistic projections suggest.

## Risks Marketers Must Factor In

**Pricing without benchmarks.** Early inventory in unproven channels commands premium pricing that cannot be evaluated against any established performance baseline. There is no floor and no ceiling that data supports.

**Brand safety without infrastructure.** A conversational environment generates infinite potential contexts. The tools to audit where a brand appears, and to prevent placement in off-brand or unflattering conversations, are not yet mature.

**Attribution gaps.** Without a clear, auditable path from ad exposure to conversion, performance teams have no mechanism for optimisation. Spending without attribution is brand investment by definition — and should be signed off accordingly, not hidden inside a performance budget.

**Reputational variance.** Appearing in an unexpected conversational context in a high-visibility AI product carries reputational exposure that most brands have not modelled in their media planning.

## Practical Recommendation

The right posture is structured attention, not urgency.

- **Define your measurement floor before you spend.** Know exactly what attribution requirements you would need before committing test budget. If you cannot define success, you cannot evaluate the test.
- **Monitor case study data as it emerges.** When early participants publish results — or when independent reporting captures benchmark performance — re-evaluate with actual data rather than projections.
- **Consider a small, budget-capped pilot if your vertical is a natural fit.** High-consideration categories with strong conversational intent — e-commerce, travel, financial services, professional services — have the structural match. Cap the test at an amount that cannot materially affect quarterly performance.
- **Do not reallocate from proven channels.** Retail media, performance search, and paid social are delivering measurable, attributable returns. No exploratory test in an unproven channel justifies reducing spend where attribution is clean.

## AI Assistants as Intent-Driven Media Surfaces

The longer-term picture is important to hold separately from the near-term tactical reality.

AI assistants are becoming the interface layer between users and decisions. That position — sitting inside the moment when someone is actively working out what to buy, where to travel, or who to hire — is structurally more valuable than almost any other media surface that currently exists.

The advertising infrastructure will mature. Measurement frameworks will emerge. Pricing will become more rational as supply increases and demand is calibrated against actual performance data.

The brands that understand the format before that maturation happens — what creative resonates inside a conversation, how users engage with commercial content in a dialogue interface, where the intent signals are strongest — will carry a compounding advantage into the period when the channel is ready to absorb real budget.

That is not an argument for spending now. It is an argument for paying close attention.
