## Andromeda Changed Everything

In late 2024, Meta quietly began rolling out a completely new ad retrieval engine called Andromeda. By January 2026, it powers all Facebook and Instagram ad delivery worldwide — and it's fundamentally different from what came before.

The old model: advertisers control targeting, the algorithm optimizes delivery. The new model: advertisers provide creative variety, Andromeda controls everything else.

If your Meta campaigns have felt different lately — more volatile early on, but potentially stronger once they settle — Andromeda is why.

## How Andromeda Actually Works

Andromeda is Meta's ad retrieval system — the engine that decides which ads get considered before ranking even happens. It processes over 15 million new ad creatives monthly. When a user opens their feed, Andromeda pulls from a massive pool of candidate ads based on creative signals, not just audience targeting parameters.

This means your creative is your targeting now. The visual elements, hooks, messaging angles, and formats you use determine who sees your ads — more than any interest-based targeting ever did.

## Why Your Old Strategy Is Broken

### Micro-Variations Don't Work Anymore

The old playbook of taking one winning image and testing it with dozens of different headlines is dead. Meta's visual recognition models view an image with slightly different text overlays as essentially the same image. If Andromeda perceives a lack of creative diversity, it penalizes your account with higher CPMs.

### Creative Fatigue Hits Faster

Under Andromeda, creative fatigue can set in as quickly as 10-14 days, especially with high spend or smaller audiences. The system tests aggressively, which accelerates fatigue cycles. When frequency exceeds 3.0 and CTR drops 30% or more, you need fresh creative immediately.

### New Metrics Matter

Meta rolled out Creative Fatigue and Creative Similarity scores. High Creative Similarity directly raises your CPMs because Andromeda treats repetitive content as less valuable. Until you fully understand these new scores, treat rising CPMs as your primary red flag.

## The New Playbook for 2026

### 1. Launch 10-15 Conceptually Distinct Assets

Your creative library needs genuine diversity — not just variations. Include static images, short-form raw video, founder selfies, polished production video, GIFs, memes, and carousels. Each piece should represent a different creative angle or hook.

### 2. Simplify Your Campaign Structure

Consolidate into fewer campaigns with broader targeting and combined budgets. Andromeda learns faster with consolidated data. The winning structure in 2026: one campaign, one broad ad set, and a growing library of varied creatives.

### 3. Combine Breadth with Iteration

Creative diversity is only half the equation. You also need to iterate on winning concepts to extend their impact. Without variants, even strong concepts fatigue quickly. Without diversity at the concept level, variants become incremental tweaks that never move the needle.

### 4. Use This Creative Framework

The most effective approach combines Meta's native AI tools with strategic planning:

- Create 8-12 core concepts manually with different hooks, angles, and formats
- Use Meta AI tools to generate 2-3 variations per concept
- Enable Advantage+ Creative on all ads
- Track creative-level performance weekly
- Feed learnings into your next creative batch every 2 weeks

### 5. Don't Reset the Learning Phase

Refrain from adjusting ad elements too often — frequent edits reset the learning phase and interrupt Andromeda's pattern recognition. Before launching new campaigns, decide on a minimum no-touch window (one week or 50-75 conversions) where you commit to no changes unless something is truly broken.

### 6. Embrace Advantage+ Defaults

As of early 2026, Advantage+ campaigns are the default for Sales, Leads, and App Promotion objectives. Advantage+ Audience, Placements, Creative enhancements, and campaign budget optimization are all pre-selected. Meta strongly encourages keeping these defaults on — and the data supports it.

## Attribution Changed Too

Engaged-view attribution is now standard with the threshold dropping from 10 seconds to 5. Incremental attribution — which lets you optimize for conversions that only happened because of your ad — is available and represents a significant shift in how Meta measures campaign value.

## The Bottom Line

Andromeda performs best when paired with thoughtful, consistent, human-led creative strategy. Advertisers following these practices report 20-35% higher ROAS compared to those running legacy campaign structures.

Your competitive advantage in 2026 isn't in targeting settings — it's in creative velocity, strategic discipline, and the quality of your post-click experience.
